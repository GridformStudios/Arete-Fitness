00Start here

Overview & the stack.

By the end of this guide, your Arete site will accept card payments, take bookings, show a live schedule, run a referral program, and let you keep track of everyone who buys or books.

The accounts you'll need

  • Google account — for your business calendar. You may already have one; a dedicated Arete Fitness account is cleaner.
  • Stripe — for accepting card payments. Fees: 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction.
  • Calendly — for taking bookings. Free tier works for now; paid tier (~$12/mo) unlocks more.
Hosting is handled Your developer is taking care of web hosting and your domain — you don't need to set up or pay for that yourself. When this guide says “push it live,” that just means handing off the updated file (or committing it, if you've been given repo access).
Costs Free to start. All accounts above have free tiers. The only unavoidable cost is Stripe's per-transaction fee — you pay nothing until a customer pays you. Calendly's paid tier is optional (~$12/mo) and only worth it if the free tier's limits become a problem.

Why this order

These phases build on each other. Google Calendar has to exist before Calendly can sync to it. Stripe products have to exist before you can create payment links. Payment links have to exist before your CTA buttons can point to them. Don't skip ahead — you'll get stuck.

Time budget Set aside a full weekend day, or three weeknight evenings. Phase 2 (Stripe) has a verification wait of 1–3 business days baked in — start it early and do other phases while you wait.

What we're intentionally not building

See Appendix: What NOT to worry about. Short version: no custom user accounts, no email marketing automation, no analytics dashboards. You're starting a small local gym, not launching Peloton.

01Foundation

Set up your business Google Calendar.

This is your source of truth for when you're teaching, when you're available, and when you're off. Everything else points to it.

Time
10 minutes
You'll need
A Google account
Why now
Calendly and your website both point to this calendar

Create the calendar

  1. Go to calendar.google.com and sign in.
  2. In the left sidebar, find "Other calendars" and click the + next to it.
  3. Choose "Create new calendar".
  4. Fill in:
    • Name: Arete Fitness Training
    • Description: Training sessions, availability, and events for Arete Fitness
    • Time zone: (GMT-08:00) Pacific Time - Los Angeles
  5. Click Create calendar. Give it a few seconds — it now appears in your sidebar under "My calendars".

Make it public so your website can display it

  1. In the left sidebar, hover over Arete Fitness Training.
  2. Click the three-dot menu that appears, then "Settings and sharing".
  3. Scroll to "Access permissions for events".
  4. Check "Make available to public". Choose "See all event details" so people can read what each event is about (not just "busy").
  5. Scroll down to "Integrate calendar".
  6. Find "Calendar ID" — it looks like a long email address ending in @group.calendar.google.com. Copy this whole string and paste it somewhere safe. You'll need it later.
Privacy check "Public" here means anyone with the link can see events on this calendar. Don't put personal appointments here — this is Arete-only. Keep your personal calendar separate.

Save these details somewhere safe

Keep a note (in a password manager, or a "Arete Business Info" doc) with:

  • Google account email: ______________________
  • Google Calendar ID: ______________________

You'll paste this Calendar ID into Calendly and into your website. Both places.

02Payments

Create your Stripe account.

Stripe is how your website will accept card payments. Signup is quick; verification takes a couple of days, so start this early.

Time
20 min signup, 1–3 business days verification
You'll need
Business bank account, tax ID (EIN or SSN), address, government ID
Cost
Free account; 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction

Sign up

  1. Go to stripe.com and click "Start now".
  2. Enter your Arete business email and a strong password.
  3. Verify your email (they send a link).
  4. When you land in the Stripe dashboard, you'll see an "Activate payments" or "Complete your account" banner. Click it.
  5. Fill in the business details:
    • Business type: Individual / Sole Proprietor if you haven't formed an LLC yet, LLC if you have
    • Business address: your studio address (26923 Fuerte Dr) or your home address
    • Tax ID: your EIN if you have one, otherwise your SSN
    • Bank account: routing + account number for where deposits should go
  6. Upload a government ID when prompted.
  7. Submit for verification. Stripe reviews and gets back to you within 1–3 business days.
Don't wait to move on While Stripe verifies, keep going with the next phases. You can create products and payment links in Test mode immediately — they just can't accept real money until Stripe approves. Toggle "Test mode" in the top-right of the Stripe dashboard.

03Payments

Create products and payment links.

A Payment Link is a URL from Stripe that takes a customer to a checkout page for a specific product. No coding required. You'll create one link per thing you sell, then paste them into your site.

Time
30–45 minutes for all products
You'll need
Stripe account (test mode is fine)
Ceiling
Payment Links can't enforce bundle usage; that's manual (Phase 8)

Create the products

  1. In the Stripe dashboard, click Product catalog in the left sidebar (or go to dashboard.stripe.com/products).
  2. Click "+ Add product".
  3. For each product you sell, fill in:
    • Name (e.g., "8-Session Bundle")
    • Description (e.g., "8 group or private sessions. Valid 90 days.")
    • Price
    • Recurring or one-time (see below)
  4. Save the product.
  5. Repeat for every product below.

Your product list

ProductPriceBilling
Intro Session$15One-time
Drop-in Session$35One-time
Private 1-on-1 Session$60One-time
Summer Drop-in (August only)$20One-time
4-Session Bundle$130One-time
8-Session Bundle$245One-time
12-Session Bundle$350One-time
Monthly Membership$200Recurring — monthly
Recurring vs. one-time Only the Monthly Membership is recurring. Everything else is one-time. Get this wrong and your customer either gets billed forever for a bundle, or has to re-pay each month for their membership.

Create a Payment Link for each product

  1. From the product page, click "Create payment link".
  2. Set:
    • Quantity: fixed to 1 (customer can't buy 12 intro sessions at once)
    • Collect customer info: Name + Email (required — that's how you'll know who paid)
    • After payment: show a confirmation page, or redirect to a "Thanks" page on your site
  3. Click Create link.
  4. Copy the URL. It'll look something like: 
    https://buy.stripe.com/aBc123XyZ
  5. Paste it into a notes doc, labeled with the product name.
  6. Repeat for every product.

Store your links safely

04Integration

Put payment links on your site.

Right now, every "Book" and "Reserve" button on the site points to the Contact page. You'll swap those to point at Stripe checkout instead.

Time
15 minutes
You'll need
Your Stripe payment link URLs, a text editor for the HTML file
Difficulty
Find & replace

What to change

In the HTML file, find each pricing button that currently looks like:

<a class="btn btn-secondary" data-nav="contact">Reserve 8-pack</a>

And change it to:

<a class="btn btn-secondary" href="https://buy.stripe.com/YOUR_8PACK_LINK" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Reserve 8-pack</a>

Key changes:

  • Remove data-nav="contact" (that was for internal navigation).
  • Add href="..." with the real Stripe link.
  • Add target="_blank" rel="noopener" — this opens checkout in a new tab so people don't lose your site.

All the buttons to update

WhereButton labelPoints to
Individual Sessions pageBook introIntro Session link
Individual Sessions pageBook privatePrivate 1-on-1 link
Bundles pageReserve 4-pack4-Session Bundle link
Bundles pageReserve 8-pack8-Session Bundle link
Bundles pageReserve 12-pack12-Session Bundle link
Bundles pageStart membershipMonthly Membership link
The drop-in is different The "See schedule" button on the Drop-in card intentionally leads to the schedule page — not directly to checkout. Drop-in customers usually want to see if there's a session at a time they can make, THEN pay. When you add Calendly (Phase 5), that button flow becomes: See schedule → click session → Calendly → pay via Stripe. Don't attach a payment link to the "See schedule" button.

Save, upload, and test

  1. Save the HTML file after your edits.
  2. Push your changes so they go live (your developer is handling hosting — hand them the updated file, or commit it if you have repo access).
  3. Visit each pricing button on the live site. Click. Confirm it opens the right Stripe checkout in a new tab.
  4. If Stripe is still in test mode, complete a test purchase with card 4242 4242 4242 4242 to make sure the flow works end-to-end.
  5. Once Stripe verifies your account, flip out of test mode. Real payments start flowing.
05Booking

Set up Calendly booking.

Calendly lets people book time on your calendar without emailing you. It syncs to Google Calendar so it knows when you're free.

Time
30 minutes
You'll need
Your Google Calendar from Phase 1
Cost
Free tier limited; paid is ~$12/mo
Calendly's real limits for a gym Calendly is designed for one-on-one appointments. It does not track "you've used 3 of your 8 bundle sessions" — that's manual for now (see Phase 8). It also can't take payments directly on the free tier. What it CAN do is let people pick a time and put it on your calendar. That's the piece worth solving here.

Sign up and connect Google Calendar

  1. Go to calendly.com and sign up with your Arete email.
  2. During onboarding, choose "Connect Google Calendar" and sign in with the same Google account you used in Phase 1.
  3. When it asks which calendar to check for conflicts, select Arete Fitness Training (and your personal calendar, so double-bookings don't happen).
  4. When it asks which calendar to add new bookings to, select Arete Fitness Training.
  5. Set your timezone: Pacific Time.

Set your general availability

  1. Go to the Availability tab (top nav).
  2. Set your working hours. Example: Mon–Fri 6am–8pm, Sat 8am–12pm.
  3. Add buffer time before/after appointments: 15 minutes each. Prevents back-to-back stress.
  4. Set minimum scheduling notice: 12 hours. Nobody books a 6am session at 4:30am.

Create your Event Types

An "Event Type" is a bookable service. You'll create two.

Private 1-on-1 Session

  1. Click + Create → One-on-One.
  2. Name: Private 1-on-1 Session
  3. Duration: 45 minutes
  4. Location: In person, address 26923 Fuerte Dr, Lake Forest, CA 92630
  5. Availability: whenever your general availability is open
  6. Custom questions: Ask "What are you hoping to work on?" so you can prep
  7. Save. Copy the booking link — looks like calendly.com/yourname/private-session

Group Session (drop-in / bundle use)

Group sessions on Calendly's free tier are limited. Two options:

  • Option A: Use "Group Event" (free tier allows this) — one-time slot with a capacity limit (e.g., 8 attendees). Good for one-off classes but you have to create each time slot manually.
  • Option B: Upgrade to Calendly Standard ($12/mo) which has better recurring group booking.

For starting out, Option A is fine. You'll create a group event for each recurring time slot:

  1. Click + Create → Group Event.
  2. Name: Mon 6am Strength & Conditioning (specific — you'll create one per weekly slot)
  3. Duration: 60 minutes
  4. Location: In person, address as above
  5. Maximum invitees: capacity of the room (say, 8)
  6. Available times: Only Mondays at 6am (use Calendly's "Set specific date ranges" or repeat weekly)
  7. Save. Copy the link.
  8. Repeat for each weekly slot: Mon 5:30pm, Wed 6am, Fri 6am, Sat 9am, etc.
Faster alternative If creating 5 separate weekly slots feels tedious, consider Acuity Scheduling instead of Calendly — it handles recurring class schedules better out of the box. Costs about the same. Just know that if you switch tools, you'll re-do this phase.
06Integration

Embed the Google Calendar on your site.

The Schedule page currently shows a demo calendar (US Holidays). You'll swap it for your real Arete Fitness Training calendar.

Time
10 minutes
You'll need
Your Calendar ID from Phase 1
Difficulty
Copy-paste one URL

Build your embed URL

Take your Calendar ID (from Phase 1). It should look like:

abc123xyz456@group.calendar.google.com

Now build this URL, replacing YOUR_CALENDAR_ID with yours — but URL-encoded (replace @ with %40):

https://calendar.google.com/calendar/embed?src=YOUR_CALENDAR_ID&ctz=America%2FLos_Angeles&mode=MONTH&wkst=1&hl=en&showTitle=0&showNav=1&showPrint=0&showTabs=1&showCalendars=0&showTz=0

Example: if your Calendar ID is arete123@group.calendar.google.com, replace it in the URL as arete123%40group.calendar.google.com.

Easier way In Google Calendar → Settings for your Arete calendar → scroll to "Integrate calendar" → find "Embed code". Google gives you a full <iframe> ready to copy. Just take the src="" value from it and drop it in place of the demo URL. Add &mode=MONTH if it's not already there.

Swap the URL on your site

  1. Open the HTML file in a text editor.
  2. Find the Schedule page section (search for id="page-schedule").
  3. You'll see an <iframe> with a src="..." pointing to the US Holidays calendar.
  4. Replace that src value with your Arete calendar's URL from above.
  5. Save the file and push it live (hand it to your developer, or commit it if you have repo access).
  6. Visit the Schedule page. You should see your calendar — empty at first, since you haven't added events yet.

Add events to your calendar

Every training session, private appointment, and closure day gets added to Arete Fitness Training in Google Calendar. Recurring events save time:

  1. Open Google Calendar.
  2. Click a time slot (say, Monday 6am).
  3. Name: Group Strength & Conditioning
  4. Guests: leave empty (this is a public class, not an invited meeting)
  5. Location: paste the studio address
  6. Under "Does not repeat", change to Weekly on Monday. Choose end date "Never" or set an end date.
  7. Save.

Repeat for each recurring session. Adjust individual instances (e.g., if you're closed a specific week) by clicking that event and choosing "This event" vs "Following events".

Don't remove the placeholder note yet Until your calendar has real events, the placeholder note under the calendar helps visitors understand what they're looking at. Once you have events populated, edit the HTML and remove that placeholder note.
07Growth

Refer-a-friend using Stripe promo codes.

Your program: friend gets $5 off their first purchase, referrer gets 20% off their next. Stripe promo codes handle the discount math. You handle the referral tracking manually.

Time
20 minutes to set up, ongoing tracking
You'll need
Stripe active, a Google Sheet for tracking
Automation ceiling
Real automated referrals need paid tools (see below)

Create the two promo codes in Stripe

Friend's $5 off code

  1. In Stripe dashboard, go to Products → Coupons (or search "coupons" in the top bar).
  2. Click + New coupon.
  3. Fill in:
    • Type: Amount off — $5.00 USD
    • Duration: Once (only applies to one purchase)
    • ID: FRIEND5
    • Maximum redemptions: leave blank (unlimited) or cap it if you want to control cost
  4. Click Create coupon.
  5. Now create a Promotion code for it — Coupons are internal, Promo codes are what customers type at checkout. Click "Create promotion code" attached to the coupon.
  6. Code: FRIEND5. Set active. Save.

Referrer's 20% off code

  1. Same process. + New coupon.
  2. Type: Percentage off — 20%
  3. Duration: Once
  4. ID: REFER20
  5. Create the promo code with the same name REFER20.
  6. Save.

Enable promo codes on your Payment Links

By default, Payment Links don't accept promo codes. Turn that on:

  1. Go to Payments → Payment Links.
  2. Click each of your payment links.
  3. Under "Advanced options", check "Allow promotion codes".
  4. Save. Repeat for every link.

The workflow — no code, just process

Since Stripe doesn't know who referred whom, you track it:

  1. Existing customer says "I want to refer my friend."
  2. You tell them: "Great — have your friend use code FRIEND5 at checkout. It gives them $5 off. Once they've paid, message me and I'll give you your 20% code for your next purchase."
  3. Your friend uses FRIEND5, gets $5 off.
  4. You confirm the payment (Stripe → Payments → search their email or name).
  5. You message the referrer: "Your friend signed up! Use code REFER20 on your next purchase for 20% off. It's active until [date, say 60 days from now]."
  6. Log both in your tracking sheet (see below).
Why this manual — you can't automate on the cheap Real automated referrals (unique codes per referrer, auto-issue rewards) need paid tools like GrowSurf or Referral Rock — $50–200/month. For a small local gym, manual is fine until you have 50+ regular customers. Then reconsider.

Track referrals in a Google Sheet

Simple template with these columns:

DateReferrerFriendFriend paidREFER20 issuedREFER20 used
2026-08-05Jane D.Sarah M.Yes ($15 intro)Yes (2026-08-06)No

Once a month, review this sheet. If a referrer hasn't used their 20% code within 60 days, drop them a friendly reminder.

Pricing incentive check Because your 20% referral perk now matches the 6-person group discount and beats the 4-person one (10%), a customer with 4 friends is better off using the referral than the group discount. That's fine — but you might see fewer 4-person group signups as a result. Watch for this in the first few months.
08Operations

Track customers, bundles, and attendance.

Two questions to always be able to answer: Who has paid me for what? and Who has sessions left in their bundle? The first is Stripe. The second is a spreadsheet.

Time
30 min to set up, 5 min per week to maintain
You'll need
Google Sheets, Stripe, Calendly
Ceiling
Manual bundle tracking scales to ~50 clients. Then migrate (see Appendix)

Where each piece of data lives

DataLives inHow to find it
Who paid, when, how muchStripeDashboard → Customers
Who booked what sessionCalendly + Google CalendarCalendly Scheduled Events + your calendar
Bundle sessions remainingYour Google SheetYou maintain manually
Attendance (who showed)Your Google SheetYou mark after each session

Build a client tracking sheet

Create a new Google Sheet: Arete Clients 2026. Tabs:

Tab 1 — Clients

One row per client. Columns:

  • Name
  • Email (from Stripe checkout)
  • Phone
  • Emergency contact
  • Signed waiver (Yes/No + date)
  • First session date
  • Notes (injuries, goals, anything relevant)

Tab 2 — Bundle Balances

One row per active bundle. Columns:

  • Client name
  • Bundle type (4-pack, 8-pack, 12-pack, Monthly)
  • Purchase date
  • Expiration date (90 days from purchase, or end of month for monthly)
  • Sessions purchased
  • Sessions used
  • Sessions remaining
  • Stripe payment reference (link to Stripe payment)

Tab 3 — Attendance Log

One row per session attended. Columns:

  • Date
  • Session (e.g., "Mon 6am S&C")
  • Client name
  • Payment source (drop-in / bundle / monthly)
  • Notes (e.g., "missed warm-up", "first time on deadlifts")

Weekly routine (5 minutes)

  1. After each session, jot attendees in the Attendance Log tab.
  2. For each attendee using a bundle, decrement their "Sessions remaining" in the Bundle Balances tab.
  3. Once a week, check Stripe Payments for new purchases. Add new bundles to the Bundle Balances tab.

Monthly reconciliation (15 minutes)

  1. Open Stripe Payments and filter to last month.
  2. Total: does it match what your sheet says clients bought?
  3. Check for bundles nearing expiration (within 30 days, sessions still remaining). Message those clients.
  4. Check Monthly Memberships — anyone who cancelled? Anyone whose card failed? Follow up.
  5. Cross-check attendance total with sessions used across all bundles. If off by more than a couple, find the error.
Automation option Once you're comfortable, Zapier or Make.com can watch for new Stripe payments and automatically create rows in your Google Sheet. Costs about $20/mo. Worth it around client #20 or #30 when you stop wanting to type Stripe data into a sheet by hand.
Appendix A

Troubleshooting.

The Stripe payment link opens, but the price is wrong

You probably edited the product in Stripe after creating the payment link, but the link cached the old price. Delete the payment link and create a new one from the updated product.

Calendly says "This event type isn't available"

Your Google Calendar has an all-day event blocking the day, or your general availability doesn't cover that time. Check Google Calendar for blockers.

The calendar embed on my site shows "Not found" or is blank

Two likely causes: (1) You forgot to make the calendar public (Phase 1, step 4). Go back and re-check. (2) Your Calendar ID isn't URL-encoded correctly — the @ must be %40. Double-check the src URL.

Someone paid but didn't book — how do I know?

You don't automatically. In Stripe, add a "success URL" to each payment link that redirects them to your Calendly booking page after payment. That way people who buy are pushed to book immediately.

A referral code got shared publicly and now everyone's using it

In Stripe, edit the promotion code and set a Max redemptions. Or deactivate the code and issue individually named ones per referrer (like REFER20-JANE).

My drop-in customer paid for a "Drop-in" but wants to use their session next Tuesday

That's fine — a drop-in is a one-time payment for one session, timing is up to them. Log it in your Attendance sheet when they show up.

Appendix B

When to migrate to an all-in-one platform.

The stack in this guide (Stripe + Calendly + Google Calendar + Sheet) is deliberately simple and cheap. It has a real ceiling. Signs you've hit it:

  • You have more than 30–50 regular clients and manually tracking bundle balances takes more than 30 min/week.
  • Clients are frustrated they can't self-serve — they want to log in, see their remaining sessions, book, and pay all in one place.
  • You're hiring another coach and need to track which coach ran which session, and pay them accordingly.
  • You want automated no-show / late-cancellation fees — hard to enforce manually.
  • You want a native mobile app for clients.

Platforms to look at

  • Punchpass — cheapest ($40–80/mo). Focused specifically on class packs / punch cards. Good fit if bundles are your main product.
  • Acuity Scheduling (Squarespace) — $23–50/mo. Better than Calendly for recurring class scheduling.
  • WellnessLiving — $80–200/mo. Full-featured. Owns bookings, payments, memberships, client portal.
  • Mindbody — $130+/mo. The industry standard. Expensive. Only worth it at real scale.
  • Vagaro — $30–90/mo. Popular alternative to Mindbody, cheaper.
Migration reality Migrating clients, bundles, and history to a new platform is annoying. Do it once, do it well, and try to pick a platform you'll use for years. Get a demo of two or three before committing. Your existing Stripe account can usually be connected to any of them.
Appendix C

What NOT to worry about right now.

Every one of these is a real thing you'll eventually want. None of them is worth setting up in the first six months. Focus on the guide above; come back to these when the basics are humming.

Custom client accounts / login system

Building "sign up, log in, see your bundle balance" is a real backend project (weeks of work). All-in-one platforms (Appendix B) include this. Don't build it yourself.

Automated email marketing

Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Klaviyo. Real value when you have 100+ people to email. Skip until then. Meanwhile, a monthly BCC'd email from your Gmail is fine.

Analytics dashboards

Google Analytics, Plausible, Fathom. Nice to know how many people visit the site, but you don't need it to run the business. Add it after you've made your first 10 sales.

SEO and content marketing

Blog posts, keyword research, backlinks. This pays off in 6–12 months, not next week. Meanwhile, Instagram and word of mouth get you your first 20 clients.

Instagram feed on your website

Requires paid third-party embed tools (EmbedSocial, LightWidget, ~$10/mo). Skip until people specifically ask.

Custom domain email

Nice to have (hello@aretefitness.com is more professional than areteFitness@gmail.com). Google Workspace is $7/user/mo. Do it once you have paying customers.
